# Crypto News Monitoring - February 6-8, 2026

## Summary
Bitcoin experienced a significant sell-off, erasing all post-election gains from Trump's November 2024 victory. The crypto market saw heightened volatility with thin liquidity causing sharp price movements.

## Price Movements
- **Bitcoin**: Dropped below $70,000, having given back all gains since the November 2024 election. Recovered from lows near $60,000 to around $69,000.
- **Weekly Decline**: Bitcoin fell approximately 16.5% in the 7-day period.
- **Worst Drawdown**: Described as the worst single-day drawdown since the FTX collapse.
- **Altcoins Hit Harder**:
  - Ether: -22.4%
  - BNB: -23.4%
  - Solana: -25.2%
- **Market Index**: CoinDesk 20 (CD20) lost more than 17% of its value in a week.

## Market Sentiment
- Described as a "sell at any price" rout by traders
- Options market: Implied volatility jumped to 99th percentile, with skew tilting toward unusually expensive puts
- Ether flagged as the "epicenter of the pain"
- Widespread cross-asset deleveraging observed (Nasdaq 100 tracker QQQ fell ~500bps over three sessions)

## Liquidity & Technical Factors
- **Thin Liquidity**: Market depth has shrunk significantly, causing larger price swings
- Thomas Probst (Kaiko research analyst): "This contraction has been underway for several months and remains ongoing, suggesting it is likely to persist for some time"
- "Reduced liquidity translates into sharper and more erratic price movements"
- October 2025 saw the largest crypto liquidation event in history after Trump's Chinese tariff announcements, with liquidity yet to fully return

## Regulatory & Institutional News
- **Institutional ETFs**: Spot bitcoin ETF flows turned negative
- **Exchange Operations**: Gemini planning to close operations in several regions and cut staff
- **Federal Reserve**: Kevin Warsh named as next Fed chair, expectations he could shrink the Fed's balance sheet
- **Trump Administration Crypto Policy**: The friendly stance on crypto has been tested by broader macroeconomic pressures and market volatility

## Key Quotes
- Denny Galindo (Morgan Stanley Wealth Management): "The flash crash back in the fall was this kind of pin that popped the leverage bubble"
- Jasper De Maere (Wintermute): Institutional desks reported "small but manageable liquidation," which did not fully explain the size of the move

## Macroeconomic Factors
- Investor concerns about inflated tech valuations
- Uncertain path of U.S. Federal Reserve rate cuts
- Precious metals and cryptocurrencies sold off heavily on January 30 after Kevin Warsh Fed chair announcement

## Outlook
- Liquidity expected to remain thin for the near future
- Potential for further downside if institutional demand through ETFs doesn't return
- Some forecasts still target $150,000 based on institutional demand and regulatory clarity expectations
